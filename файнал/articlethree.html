"Kazakhstan Khalkyn" is the main initiator of charitable activities

by Ninbay Shu Ga | 26 December 2023

The Kazakhstan Khalkina Public Foundation is at the forefront of philanthropic work in Kazakhstan, focusing on various aspects of social welfare and community development. In 2022, the foundation hosted a landmark national philanthropy conference, demonstrating its commitment to fostering collaboration and innovation in philanthropy. More than 40 Zoom meetings were held, bringing together representatives of 1,173 non-profit organizations to share ideas and best practices. According to the head of the public foundation, Bolat Zhamishev, the direction of the foundation’s work is not determined by board decisions, but is formed within the non-governmental sector.

One of the key factors in the fund’s success has been its ability to attract support from a variety of sources. In 2022, 60 Kazakh companies contributed a total of 131 billion tenge, demonstrating the private sector’s commitment to social responsibility. In addition, the state’s major sovereign wealth fund, Samruk-Kazyna, contributed more than 20 billion tenge to support the fund’s initiatives. Individual donors have also played a critical role, contributing 39 billion tenge to the fund’s cause. The fund has also received significant donations from the general public, demonstrating widespread support for its mission. Three construction companies recently contributed 500 million tenge to a project to provide prosthetics for low-income children and youth.

The foundation's main areas of activity are healthcare, social support, education, culture, sports and assistance to victims of emergency situations. Their programs are designed to meet the needs of communities in all 20 regions of Kazakhstan, with a special emphasis on rural areas and small towns.

"The Fund has initiated 67 charitable programs and projects throughout its history. Without funding from Kazakhstan Halkyn, their implementation for a total of 177 billion tenge would have been impossible. The resources were collected through charitable contributions. At the moment, the Fund has received 196.6 billion dollars. tenge, of which 90.9 billion tenge have been allocated for charitable activities over 2 years. To date, expenses have amounted to 90.9 billion tenge, but the projects that we have approved and are implementing are 177 billion tenge," says B. Zhamishev.
